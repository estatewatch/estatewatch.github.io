Estate Watch is back!

  • 06 November 2024

Thanks to Just Space, London Tenants Federation and student volunteers from University College London, Estate Watch is back online and back in action!

WE'RE BACK!

The website was mothballed last year when funding ran out but has now been spruced up, updated and put back online as an open source project.

The new website now has a live CSV open data feed and the ability to be collaboratively edited and managed directly through a web browser. Info on how to get involved or contribute can be found here.

In addition, we have added a number of social housing blocks earmarked for demolition but which are not technically 'estates'.

These include Brook House(Ealing), Dean Court(Ealing), Eros House(Lewisham), Neville Close(Ealing), Oaktree Court(Ealing), RAF Uxbridge(Hillingdon), Southwold Drive(Barking & Dagenham), Walbrook House(Enfield), Morden Regeneration Zone (Merton) and Mendip and Penine House above Edmonton Green Shopping Centre.

In future blog updates, we will be looking at what exactly constitutes a housing estate for the purposes of the Mayor's Estate Regeneration Policies and how these should be applied.

Most of the additions are from scouring through London boroughs' new draft Local Plans and site allocation documents where estates have been identified as potential redevelopment sites. We will also be taking a closer look at the Local Plan process of site allocations and the 'call for sites' which informs them as well as the implications this has for any subsequent meaningful consultation with residents. Watch this space!
